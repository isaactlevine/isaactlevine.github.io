Isaac Levine is a consultant specializing in machine learning and protocol technologies. He also has an extensive background in Chinese political economy and philosophy.

He collaborates with companies, nonprofits, and artists by strategizing and building products/experiences in the following problem-spaces:

- using machine learning systems to support open-ended processes and augmentation of thinking
- using machine learning to learn more about oneself and the feedback loops entailed; virtual beings
- using machine learning for creative purposes, and in particular producing 'out of distribution' or highly novel outputs
- how protocol-thinking can help us design novel modes of communication, information, and identity in networked contexts

Currently: tofth.org, a strategy agency for R&D teams in tech.
